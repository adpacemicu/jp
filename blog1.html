Call for Submissions!

Ad Pacem would like to help you show the world your creativity and finesse in writing!

Have you ever?

thought of a great idea but never had the chance to share it to everyone? Ad Pacem is here for that reason; to help you further that idea and be able to project it to a bigger audience or turn it into a reality.

Enhance your writing skills

Ad Pacem members are composed with equipped members in the fields of arts, science, literature and technology. From practicing lawyers to web developers, the diverse community that we have will be able to help you write a paper of your interests may it be a thesis, short essay, blog content or even a proposal .

Show the world your ideas

With Ad Pacem's connection, what we aim to do is show your work to a wide audience through workshops, lecture or creative sessions such as presentations or simply a brown bag session. Picture this; you have an idea to change something through your paper and someone out there wants that idea to be heard to turn it into a reality, think of Ad Pacem as a bridge, we are here to help you along the way. 

Guidelines

1

FORMATTING GUIDELINES

​Contents: Abstract, paper, and author bio.
Font and Spacing: Times New Roman, size 12, line spacing 1.5.
Page: A4 paper with margins 2.5cm on top and bottom and 3cm on left and right sides.

Submit your paper in Word or Google Docs format to adpacem.icu@gmail.com.

2

BIO GUIDELINES

In around 100 words, include your name, explain your background, including university, degree, applicable work experience), and other relevant research interests.

The same restrictions apply to papers with joint papers, but if there were one or more main authors, that should be noted. 

3

ABSTRACT & PAPER GUIDELINES

Abstract: 150 words or less with a brief outline of your research question, findings, and conclusion.

Introduction: Explain your research question in the context of public policy and peace studies as well as a thesis statement.

Literature Review: Give the context and academic background of your topic.

Methodology: Explain your variables, related terms, and framework as well as outline gaps in the field that warrant this research. Include reasons why your research contributes to the field.

Analysis: Interpret your data within the theoretical structure you provided.

Summary: Provide a conclusion that explains the results of your findings in brief, areas that still need further research given your findings.

Bibliography: Cite your sources according to APA format. 

Submissions

Submission for journals comes into 2 rounds: Winter and Summer

1

WINTER DEADLINE

Articles must be submitted by March 15, 2019

Those who are selected for initial peer review will be notified by end of March 2019.

If there are any further edits to papers those will be requested in April 2019.

Papers will be finalized by the committees then.

Those who submit to the winter issue will be able to view their articles online in May 2019

2

SUMMER DEADLINE

Articles must be submitted by June 30, 2019.

Those who are selected for initial peer review will be notified in July 2019.

If there are any further edits to papers those will be requested in July 2019 .

Papers will be finalized by the committee in August and September.

Those who submit to the summer issue will be able to view their articles online in September 2019.
